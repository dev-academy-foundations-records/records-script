Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

• Research Neuroplasticity

1. Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

neuroplasticity tells people that our brains are like malleable plastic that is constantly being reshaped by our daily experiences. For example, if you keep practicing new things, you are causing different neurons to fire in concert, and it strengthens the connections between those cells. Over time, these connections become thick, hardy road maps that link various parts of the brain. Permanent learning therefore takes place.

2. Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

I would keep practicing coding. Even though I struggle to understand the logic of the code at first, I do not run away but keep repeating it. Everytime I repeat it, I stimulate the neurons to wire together and eventually build thicker, stronger, more hard-wired connections in the brain. At the end, I will find myself mastering coding.

3. What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Practice, practice and practice. Also, going out of the comfort zone and confront challenges and difficulties. Always looking for solutions rather than running away.

• Research Growth Mindset

1. Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

People with a growth mindset believe that they can get smarter and understand that effort makes them stronger. If we learners all have growth mindsets, we would not set limitations for ourselves and have a faith that I can get achievements if I put extra time and effort.

2. In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I am impressed with the power of growth mindset. Most of us are not born with high IQ or are extremely intelligent, however, it does not stop us from achieving success and happiness if we believe that our abilities can be developed through effort.

3. How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

Whenever I encounter difficulties and want to run away, I would tell myself that it is ok to get struggled, just take your time and keep trying and you will get it sorted.
