My thoughts on my learning plan are pretty much the same as before! Some new things I’ve thought of while rereading the questions:

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I guess the goal is to become a software engineer! I’m still exploring where exactly I’d want to go with it. The industry seems so broad and diverse. I’m excited to see where it takes me. Ultimately I’d like to be able to work maybe 3 or 4 days a week remotely and do some travelling.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning:

I’m nothing if not persistent! I’m very good at pushing through to find a solution or to meet a deadline. Limitations are definitely the things I’ve previously mentioned around executive functioning — focussing my attention, not procrastinating, sleeping enough, etc.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I know this isn’t what you’re really asking, but I’d like to make a friend or two! The last couple of years have been weird, so it’s been cool to start meeting people again.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I will try and work consistently to stay on top of things instead of doing everything on the last day. I will try and seek help earlier instead of spending hours Googling. Life does frequently get overwhelming, but I will let facilitators know if it gets to the point where it’s significantly impacting my work.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team:

It’s been lovely so far. You’re all so super responsive and friendly. Just keep doing you!

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things:

I did accidentally sign up to some other commitments but they're fairly flexible! Will let y'all know if anything does ever clash.
