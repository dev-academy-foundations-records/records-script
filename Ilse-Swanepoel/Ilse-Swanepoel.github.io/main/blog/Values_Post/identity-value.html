Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration


Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

There are so many ethical decisions we need to make throughout our life time. Sometimes we will look back at these and regret the decision we made, other times, these decisions will help define who we become. When ever I come across a decision I need to make, I turn the question back to me and who I want to be. I let kindness drive my decisions. Is it always the correct way to do it? No, it can bring the consequence of being taken advantage of, and can lead you to believe that others also act with the base value of kindness, even though this may not be the case. I do try and believe that all humans are inately kind and that other unkind acts come from survival and trauma instincts.

A couple of years ago, I had a student approach me. He was not my student but many of his friends were in my class. He came to me and said he was very hungry and had eaten his lunch in the morning becuase he had no breakfast. I told him we could call his mum to get some extra food brought for him. He was adamant that his mum would get angry and he would get in trouble. I then said we could call the office and organise some sandwiches and he said no, last time they did that they called his mum to let her know and he got into lots of trouble. For me, school was always meant to be a safe space for these children, where they can come to you with problems without fear of getting into trouble. I called the office and requested sandwiches, I asked them not to contact the family on this occasion but instead we would make a note to keep track of whether this occurs again or not. This meant that if neglect etc was happening at home, we could track it, and taking the neccessary steps, but that in the meantime, this boy could continue learning, continue feeling safe and feel his decisions were respected. Kindness and respect often overlap for me.



Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I believe the biggest influence for who I am today, is my parents. They left behind a wealthy life in South Africa to move here with barely any money, no jobs, no family, no friends. They both worked incredibly hard to get to where they are today and are very generous and kind people. The sacrifice they made encourages me to see the beauty of New Zealand around me, everyday. To appreciate the freedom and opportunities that have been given to me. As a result, I am a really hard worker and I try my best in everything I do, no matter how small. The kindness my parents have shown to others has shown me how this impacts your fulfullness in life. I have also seen them turn around from an extrememly racist and unkind society, to being accepting of different cultures and belief. This kindness and respct has surrounded me my entire life and I try hard to project this from my own being.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

The strengths that will support me on this journey, are my work ethic and drive to better myself. My curiosity and life long love of learning, will help me to stay comitted. My kindness and ability to work with others will potentially help others to stay committed and succeed in this course too.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

My limitation, especially my lack of organisation skills could be my downfall. This is especially true in Foundations where there is no one to keep me accountable all of it needs to be self-driven and effeciently managed. That said I was well aware of this limitation when taking on this challenge so it is something I already have plans in place for.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

When there is tension between a group, I often work to ensure everyone has the same goal in mind. Often, people have the same goals, but different ways they want to go about it. Communication needs to be clear and we need to be on the same page. One thing that is essential when working with others, is knowing you can't always have your way, even if you whole-heartedly believe your way is better. Leading by example is often a way to get others on board, especially if you have already built that repoir with them. I have often used the line "While I continue to see this differently to you, I am willing to support you in your decision as I know we have the same goal in mind - this was usually the wellbeing of the students in my class or something similar. You have to pick your battles. It is also essential to have someone who at the end of the day , will make the final call. Colaboration is important but it needs to be balanced with some kind of hierachy, even if this means to collaborators seek and expert opinion and go with that advice.
