The End...of the Beginning


What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

1. I have learnt to be content in my own presence. Usually I require people around me to talk to, bounce ideas off etc but as foundations has progressed, I found less of a need to be around people, and I have become more comfortable with my own presence.
2. Changing habits is extremely hard, even if you do have the motivation. I have found it extrememly difficult to stick to any sort of routine, and I know I am going to struggle with that once we start bootcamp.
3. I am a lot more capable than I give myself credit for. I was expecting massive meltdowns from finding things hard, but I have found that I have a lot more resilience than I ever thought.


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values for everyone will be different. Empathy in understanding that everyone is different. Some of us will prefer to work alone, in silence, others would rather have some buzzing discussions around. So understanding what everyone values and not pushing your values on them will be a part of empathy. Self-awareness- When you are struggling, you need to be aware so you can help yourself before you spiral. Knowing yourself will help prevent burn out.


What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

We never learnt these skills in high school or uni. Yet they are essential, makes me grateful that I chose Dev Academy to study with.


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Often, it can feel a bit rushed, just because you spend so much time on the programming, that core sometimes feels like you have run out of steam.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I have mentioned previously in my blog posts how important I believe these core/human skills are. Unfortunately, education in this space has been lacking for so long that many people don't understand the concepts of neuroplasticity, emotional intelligence, self-awareness etc. The understanding and practise of these skills is essential in life, but vital to something as challenging as coding. Dev Academy has made a great choice by teaching core skills alongside tech skills. We will certainly be more well-rounded programmers as a result.


Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, 100% it has not been a waste of time. Even though I have been exposed to many of the core concepts previously, refreshers and further learning are always beneficial. These skills are essential so definitely not a waste of time.
