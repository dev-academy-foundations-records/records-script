Research Neuroplasticity

  1. What is Neuroplasticity

    2. Neuroplasticity, which is also known as neural plasticity or brain plasticity, we can see it as how the brain restructure itself at cellular level. It is a recently new concept, as before, public believe brain is static after it grew. While new resarchs believe that the brain is malleable, the more individual uses the brain, stronger the conections between the neural cells are. Oppositely, lack of using specific part of brain shows the neural conection decline. This phenomenon can be seen when people experiences new things or to adapt environmental changes This reorganising an restructuring in brain is called neuroplasticity

  2. How understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people?

    3. The principles of neuroplasticity explain how factors like environments, age of individual, the engagement of acitivities affect the restructuring and reorganising of brain, knowing the pattern of how the brain plasticity works well benift people to learn new things, adapt changing environments or even deal with brain damage.

  3. How do I engage with neuroplasticity for my own benefit?

    4. The principles of neuroplasticity believes under the intensive training, with good amouth of repetition and through meaningful and relective learning, restructuring of brain will happen.

  4. Ways to improve neuroplasticity

    5. Different researches shows that learning a new particular thing, can contribute to the learning of other things, they have listed activities that can positive influence individual's brain plasticity:

    • learning new languages
    • regular do mindfulness practice, such as meditation
    • Healthy diet
    • Regular exercise
    • Connecting with people
    • Cook food with new recipes
    • Learn to play an instrument
    • Listen to different music
    • Go to museums or concerts

Research Growth Mindset

  1. What it is a growth mindset and why it is important.

    2. Dweck(2014) states that learners with the believe and understand that their abilities can be developed are having a growth mindset. To be specific the learner have a mind that their abilities are not limited by talent, their can grow by dedication, the opposite of a growth mindset is fixed mindset, which are a type of group that consider their intelligence are innat, and they personal growth is litmited. They seems avoid challenge and are easily give up compared to people with groth mindset.

    It is essential to equip with a growth mindset in this quick changing world. There are up coming situations challenge people from every aspect of daily life, no mater you are student, who wants have academic achievement, or employee who are seeking better work performance. People with a growth mind throught dedication meaningful learning, they are able to learn subjects well, or do projects with team members. As long as learner believe they will achieve without giving up, even sometime they are slow, they will finally reach the goal.
